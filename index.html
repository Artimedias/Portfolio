I'm 19, and I live on the in between from suburban and rural western Washington. I have both parents, and a younger sister who I care for very much. I'm not very good at writing about myself, so you'll have to forgive my akwardness here. I have a couple of years of coding experience under my belt, probably about a years worth of Java, and two years worth of C#. I like to play board games and video games. Why I got in coding

I got into coding when I was 11 years old, in 7th grade. My school had a video game design class, and it was by far the most fun I had ever had in a class up to this point. From that moment on I knew I wanted to design games for a living. Of course, I had thought about it before, as I grew up playing video games and making my own board games, but it always seemed like a bit of a pipe dream, something you just said to get adults off your back when they asked what you wanted to do when you grew up, like being an author, or a lawyer. But after that, it felt real. It felt attainable. Since then that dream has changed, as I realized I liked working with pretty much any code, not just video games, and learning about how terrible the video game industry could be. I still make games though, although my gamemaker days are long over, and I've since moved to unity and C#. But I know I can't just sit and make games for myself and a friend or two forever while I work at a pizza resturant for a living. I want more than that- which is why I went to college for a computer science degree. There, I failed. Despite how much I love coding, I hate math, and it was going to take me years of math classes before I could even take an intro to java course, something I knew I could already test out of. After failing pre-calc 2 three times in a row, I moved on. I was adrift for a while, until a friend of my dads suggested that I take a coding bootcamp, so I could get what I really needed to work as a software engineer without having to suffer through years of math. After months of searching, I settled on Epicodus. After spending most of what remained of my college savings, I'm now here, and so far it's been great.
